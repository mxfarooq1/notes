# Securities / RR Registration

**IMPORTANT NOTE:** Individuals must not solicit business, market themselves as an IAR or RR, or represent that they are affiliated with Transamerica Financial Advisors (TFA), until they have received written notice from TFA that they have been approved by TFA.

I want to become registered with TFA

**Below are instructions to request a Series 6–7, Series 63 and/or Series 66 exam or request a transfer to TFA (securities registration).**

Note: All new applicants who are **not** currently registered with TFA in a Registered Representative (RR) or Investment Advisor Representative (IAR) capacity must complete the TFA Onboard application.

#### Step 1: TFA Onboard Invite

- Contact a TFA Branch Office Supervisor (BOS) or TFA Regional Branch Manager (RBM) to send an invitation through the online electronic TFA Onboard platform.
- TFA Onboard is located on MyWFG.com → Menu → TFA → Join TFA

#### Step 2: Complete and Submit the TFA Onboard Application

- **Disclosures:** Include the applicable disclosure reporting page for any “Yes” answers to section 14 and include any supporting court documentation.
- **OBAs:** Disclose any outside business activities (OBAs) for any outside activities involving direct or indirect compensation, ownership or control of the activity.
- **Payment:** Make payment through [Payment Central](https://www.mywfg.com/PaymentEngine) on MyWFG.
- **Fingerprints:**
    -    • Log on to the website: [BrokerFP.com](http://www.brokerfp.com/)
    -    • Create a username and password to access this secure site.
    -    • Enter the Fieldprint code: **FPTransAmerFinancialAP** (please note: access code is case sensitive)

  

- **Submit:** Submit your completed TFA Onboard Application

#### Step 3: TFA Onboard Application Review and Submit

- Your RBM will review your application for completeness and submit it to TFA Registration.

  

- **Background Ordered:** When payment is received, TFA Registration will request your background check.
-    • **Background:** When the background is received, a copy will be mailed to the applicant.

#### Step 4: Application is Reviewed by TFA

- Depending on the needs, Supervision, Compliance, and TFA Registration will review your application.

#### Step 5: Completed Application — TFA submits to FINRA to open exam windows or register transferring licenses

- Once accepted by FINRA, an email is sent to the applicant and Branch with the applicable exam window.
- An email is sent to the applicant and Branch when FINRA registration is approved by both FINRA and the State being registered.

#### Step 6: Complete Required Training

- **Registered Representatives (RRs) must complete:**
    -    • TFA New Representative Training course
- **Investment Advisor Representatives (IARs) must complete:**
    -    • TFA New Representative Training course
    -    • TFA New IAR Training course

#### Step 7: TFA Approval

Once TFA approves your registration, you will:

-    • Receive an approval letter (emailed to the applicant and the branch)
-    • IAR representatives must immediately complete the ADV Part 2B brochure, located in [InForms](https://www.mywfg.com/informs)
-    • Set up your TFAconnect email account

-       ○ As a TFA Financial Professional, you are assigned a TFAConnect.com email account that you must use for all securities and business-related electronic communication. You should check your TFAconnect email daily as all firm notices will be sent to this email address. You cannot forward your TFAconnect emails to your personal email account(s).

-       ○ Your email address will be in the format of: firstname.lastname@tfaconnect.com, e.g., john.smith@tfaconnect.com.

**FINRA Exam Requests**  
All FINRA exam requests must be made in writing and submitted to [TFA Registration](mailto:TFARegistration@transamerica.com). When requesting a FINRA exam after failing an exam or when the exam window closes, a written request must be submitted within one (1) to two (2) calendar days from the failed exam date or the date the window closed, or there may be an additional $125 FINRA fee and/or state fee incurred. This applies to Series 6, Series 63, Series 66, and Series 7 exams. Please note that all exam windows are good for one (1) exam attempt; the window automatically expires at the time the exam is taken.

**Important:** An individual has approximately 180 calendar days from the date his/her U4 is submitted to FINRA to successfully pass the exam or have an open exam window, otherwise, the individual will be purged from FINRA’s system. The individual will be terminated within 120 days from being purged if the required registration and exam requests have not been received by Registration and submitted to FINRA. If termed with FINRA, the individual will need to complete the TFA Onboard application, get fingerprinted and pay all registration fees. Registration/Exam fees are paid through [Payment Central](https://www.mywfg.com/PaymentEngine) on MyWFG.com.
