### 1. Licensing

### _Definitions:_

- Agents are also known as producers.
- Any individual or business entity that acts as an insurance producer must be licensed by the state.
- An agent must be appointed by the insurer(s) that the agent represents.
- A home state is defined as the state in which the applicant maintains a principal place of residence or business and is licensed to act as an insurance producer.
- A resident licensee is one who has declared New York as their home state.
- A domestic insurer is one that is domiciled in this state.
- An alien insurer is an insurer domiciled in a foreign country.
- A foreign insurer is an insurer domiciled in another state.
- An agent is a person who solicits, negotiates, or sells insurance on behalf of an insurer.
- Selling refers to the exchange of an insurance contract for money.
- Soliciting refers to the attempt to sell insurance.

### _Process:_

- To qualify for a New York resident license, an applicant must be at least 18 years old, complete any necessary prelicensing education, pass the appropriate exam (if required), apply to the department for a license within 2 years of passing the exam, and pay the required licensing fee.
- Candidates for a life and health agent or broker license must complete 40 hours of required prelicensing education unless they hold the CLU (Chartered Life Underwriter) designation.
- For applicants who are currently licensed or who have been licensed in another state and are relocating to New York, the exam and education requirements may be waived if they submit a Letter of Clearance stating that they have been licensed in good standing in their prior state within the last 90 days.
- In addition to their state life insurance license, producers need a federal FINRA securities license in order to sell variable products such as variable life or variable annuities.

### _Types of Licenses:_

- An agent is an individual authorized by an insurer to solicit or negotiate insurance.
- Agents represent the insurers, not the insured.
- An insurance broker is any person that solicits, negotiates, or sells insurance on behalf of an insured.
- An insurance consultant is any person who receives a fee from a client for providing insurance consultation or advice.
- Consultants may not sell, solicit, or negotiate insurance unless they are licensed as an agent.
- A consultant's compensation must be based upon a written agreement signed by the client.
- An adjuster must be licensed as an independent adjuster or a public adjuster.
- Independent adjusters adjust claims on behalf of an insurer.
- Public adjusters adjust claims on behalf of an insured.
- A nonresident licensee is licensed as a resident producer in a state other than New York (has a different home state) and who is permitted to transact insurance in this state.
- Nonresident licenses are based upon reciprocal agreements between states and may be issued without exam if the applicant is properly licensed in their home state.
- Business entities such as corporations, partnerships, and legal liability companies may be licensed as producers. The sub-licensee named in the licensing application will be held responsible for the entities' compliance with the New York Insurance Code.  
    
- The Superintendent may issue a temporary license for up to 90 days, without exam, to the surviving next of kin of a deceased producer or a producer who becomes totally disabled.
- The Superintendent may issue a temporary license for up to 6 months, without exam, to the designee of a licensed producer entering the armed forces.
- Licensees must report to the Superintendent any administrative action taken against them in another jurisdiction or by another governmental agency in this state within 30 days of final disposition of the matter.

### _Maintenance and Duration:_

- Producer licenses expire biennially on the licensee's birthday.
- All licensed agents, brokers, consultants, and public adjusters must complete 15 hours of approved continuing education courses every 2 years.
- Although they must obtain a license, agents who sell only accident or baggage insurance are exempt from examination and continuing education requirements.
- The Insurance Superintendent requires the filing of an assumed name to be used by a producer prior to use.
- A producer may not use an assumed name unless it has first been filed with the Superintendent.
- A licensee must notify the Superintendent of a change of any address, including an email address, within 30 days of the change.
- Every insurer, upon receipt of any inquiry from the Department respecting a claim, must, within 10 business days, furnish the department with the available information requested.

### _Disciplinary Actions:_

- If, after a hearing, it is determined that a method of competition or a practice is unfair or deceptive, the Superintendent may issue an order requiring a person to cease and desist from engaging in such a practice.
- If the Superintendent finds that a licensee is guilty of fraud, the Superintendent may refuse to renew, revoke, or suspend the license but cannot place a licensee on probation.
- The Superintendent may suspend or revoke a producer's license for writing controlled business. Controlled business is defined as receiving more than 10% of commissions in a 12-month period from insurance sold to a spouse, immediate family member, or business associate.
- The Superintendent may levy a civil penalty in an amount not exceeding $5,000 plus payment of the claim against anyone who commits a fraudulent insurance act.
- All applications for insurance must contain a notice to the applicant regarding the penalties for committing a fraudulent insurance act.
- Anyone who violates a cease and desist order may be subject to a penalty of up to $5,000 per violation.

### 2. State Regulation

### _Superintendent's General Duties and Powers:_

- The Superintendent of Financial Services (Superintendent) is appointed by the Governor to preside over the New York State Department of Financial Services and enforce and administer the New York state Insurance Code.
- The Superintendent supervises the business of, and the persons providing, financial products and services, including any persons subject to the provisions of the insurance law and the banking law.
- The Superintendent has the power to conduct investigations of matters affecting the interests of consumers of financial products and services.
- The Superintendent protects users of financial products and services by reviewing complaints, making recommendations to the governor, referring matters to the attorney general, initiating consumer financial education programs, continuing and expanding the detection, investigation and prevention of insurance fraud.

### _Company Regulation:_

- Insurers must obtain a Certificate of Authority from the Superintendent authorizing or admitting them to transact insurance in this state.
- To prove solvency, authorized insurers must file audited annual financial statements with the Superintendent proving that they have adequate legal reserves to pay claims.
- It is an unfair claims settlement practice to offer to settle a claim for less than a reasonable person would expect.
- It is an unfair claims settlement practice to not attempt in good faith to bring about prompt, fair, and equitable settlement of claims where liability has become reasonably clear.
- Insurers cannot engage in unfair claims settlement practices with such frequency as to indicate a general business practice.
- Insurers must submit a certificate of appointment for all producers with the Superintendent.
- Notice of appointment must be given to the Superintendent within 15 days from the date the agency contract is executed.
- When terminating an agent's appointment for cause, insurers must submit a statement stating the facts to the Superintendent within 30 days.

### _Unfair Prohibited Practices:_

- If there isÂ reason to believe that a person has committed an unfair trade practice, the Superintendent may conduct a hearing on the matter by giving 10-days advance written notice.
- Every insurer must establish an internal department specifically designated to investigate and resolve complaints filed with the Department of Financial Services.
- It is considered misrepresentation for an agent to make misleading statements regarding past or future dividends.
- It is considered misrepresentation for an agent to make an incomplete comparison of insurance policies for the purpose of inducing someone to lapse, forfeit, or surrender a policy.
- A misrepresentation by an insured will not void the policy unless it was material in that the insurer would not have issued the policy if the facts were known.
- If an insured makes a material misrepresentation on the application, the policy may be rescinded or voided.
- Dividends may not be guaranteed.
- Advertisements cannot include untrue, deceptive or misleading statements.
- Defamation is defined as making a false written or oral statement that is derogatory to the financial condition or that affects the solvency or financial standing of an insurer.
- Insurers may not discriminate on the basis of race, color, sex, marital status, creed, national origin, or disability.
- Offering a customer an inducement to encourage him or her to purchase a policy (such as a return of commission) is an unfair and prohibited practice known as rebating.
- Articles of merchandise with a conspicuously stamped or printed advertisement of the insurer, agent or broker are not considered rebates if they are valued at $25 or less.

### _License Regulation:_

- Writing coverage on one's own life or one's family or business associates is prohibited and considered controlled business.
- Fiduciary duties include handling premiums.
- Every insurer, upon notification of a claim, must, within 15 working days, acknowledge the receipt of such notice.
- The license of each supervising or responsible person must be prominently displayed at each agent's or broker's office.
- Producers have 30 days to file written comments to the Superintendent regarding content of a notice of termination
- The Superintendent must audit the books and records of every authorized domestic insurer at least once every 5 years.
- Insurers must maintain a record for each insurance contract for 6 years after the date of policy termination.
- The Insurance Fraud Prevention Act was passed to address the potential for abuse and illegal activities in the issuance of policies and the payment of claims.
- All persons and corporations are prohibited from soliciting or negotiating an insurance or annuity contract without a license.
- Under the Consumer Privacy Regulation, no later than the time of delivery of a new policy and at least once every year thereafter insurers must give insureds a notice explaining their privacy policies.
- Under the Consumer Privacy Regulation, customers have the right to opt-out of having their information shared with unaffiliated third parties.
- Insurers must maintain a cybersecurity program designed to prevent cyberattacks and recovery procedures if one occurs.

### _Commissions and Compensation:_

- An insurer may not pay a commission to a person that is not licensed in this state.
- If a person was licensed at the time of the sale of an insurance policy, he or she may receive renewal or deferred commissions even if he or she is no longer licensed.
- The Producer Compensation Transparency regulation establishes minimum disclosure requirements regarding the role of insurance producers and their compensation.
- Under the Producer Compensation Transparency regulation, if a purchaser wants more information about the producer's compensation, the nature, amount, and source of the compensation to be received must be disclosed in a prominent writing to the purchaser and a copy must be retained by the producer for at least 3 years.
- A disclosure of producer compensation must include a description of the producer's role in the transaction and the buyer's right to request information about the producer's compensation.

### 3. Federal Regulation

- The federal Fair Credit Reporting Act states that consumer reports must be ordered in accordance with federal law.
- The federal Fair Credit Reporting Act gives customers the right to question reports made about them by consumer reporting agencies.
- A person convicted of violating the federal Fraud and False Statements Act may be fined and imprisoned up to 15 years and is prohibited from engaging in the insurance business in this state without the prior consent of the Superintendent.
