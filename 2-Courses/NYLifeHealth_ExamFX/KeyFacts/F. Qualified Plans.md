### _General Requirements:_

- A defined benefit qualified plan is structured based on a predetermined benefit amount.
- A defined contribution plan is a qualified plan.
- The coverage requirement in a qualified plan is that a broad range of employees is covered by the plan.
- Trustees must manage qualified plan assets exclusively for the benefit of the participants.

### _Plan Types, Characteristics and Purchasers:_

- The 2 most common qualified individual retirement plans are Traditional IRAs and Roth IRAs. 
- Anybody with earned income can contribute to either plan.
- Amounts contributed to qualified plans are limited by the IRS.  
    
- In a traditional IRA, the owner must start receiving plan distributions at the age of 73.
- Roth IRAs do not have the required minimum distribution rule.  
    
- A plan where an employee forgoes current pay in exchange for future benefits is known as a deferred compensation plan.
- All or part of a distribution from a qualified plan may be rolled over into an IRA without tax.
- A trustee-to-trustee rollover eliminates the withholding tax requirement.
- Keogh plans are available to self-employed sole proprietors, partners, and their employees, but they are NOT available to corporate officers.
- A Simplified Employee Pension (SEP) is a qualified plan for small employers or for the self-employed.  
    
- A SIMPLE plan is available to small businesses that employ no more than 100 employees.
- Contributions made by participants to a SIMPLE plan must be vested immediately.
- When a corporation sets up a qualified retirement plan to contribute a portion of their net income for the benefits of employees, it is known as a profit-sharing plan.
- When surrendering a 403(b) TSA for cash under age 59 ½, all the proceeds are taxable as ordinary income plus the proceeds are subject to an IRS 10% premature distribution penalty.
