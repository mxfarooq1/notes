This chapter explained long-term care insurance, including types of care, required benefits, and state-specific regulations pertaining to LTC policies. Let's recap some of the key points:

|   |   |
|---|---|
|**LONG-TERM CARE**|   |
|**Policies**|- Available as individual policies, group policies, or as riders to life insurance policies<br>- Coverage for individuals who require living assistance at home or in a nursing home facility<br>- Must provide at least 12 months of consecutive coverage in a setting other than an acute care unit of a hospital<br>- Guaranteed renewable, but insurers may increase premiums|
|**Levels of Care**|- _Skilled care_ - daily nursing and rehabilitative care provided by medical personnel<br>- _Intermediate care_ - occasional nursing or rehabilitative care provided for stable conditions that require daily medical assistance on a less frequent basis than skilled nursing care<br>- _Custodial care_ - care for a person's activities of daily living provided in an institutional setting or in the patient's home<br>- _Home health care_ - provided by registered nurses, licensed practical nurses, licensed vocational nurses, or community-based organizations like hospice in one’s home<br>- _Residential Care_ - provided while the insured resides in a retirement community<br>- _Adult day care_ - provides for functionally impaired adults on less than a 24-hour basis<br>- _Respite Care_ - provides relief to the family caregiver; adult day care centers may also provide this type of relief<br>- _Assisted living_ - provides help with nonmedical daily activities|
|**Coverage and Benefits**|- 12 consecutive months<br>- Elimination period of 30 days or more<br>- Benefit period of 2-5 years; some offering lifetime coverage<br>- Longer benefit periods result in higher premium<br>- Trigger: inability to perform ADLs (usually 2 - 3)|
|**Required Provisions**|- Must be guaranteed renewable<br>- Must offer inflation protection<br>- Free look period - 30 days<br>- Pre-existing conditions exclusion - no more than 6 months<br>- Must cover Alzheimer's disease<br>- Disclosure requirements - outline of coverage and policy summary|
