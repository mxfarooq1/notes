In this chapter you learned about life settlements. Let’s recap the key terms and definitions, as well as producer licensing and conduct regulations:

|   |   |
|---|---|
|**GENERAL CONCEPTS**|   |
|**Life Settlement**|- Owner of a life insurance contract sells a policy that is no longer needed to a third party<br>- Separate contract required|
|**Parties to a Life Settlement**|- _Owner_ - seeks to enter into a life settlement contract<br>- _Life Settlement Broker_ - solicits or negotiates life settlement contracts for compensation; must be properly licensed<br>- _Life Settlement Prov_ider - enters into a life settlement contract with the owner; must be properly licensed|
|**LICENSING REQUIREMENTS**|   |
|**Life Settlement Broker**|- Must be at least 18 years old, be trustworthy and competent, submit application, complete prelicensing education, pass licensing exam, pay required fees, and submit fingerprints<br>- Receive approval|
|**Fingerprinting**|- Required for life settlement brokers and providers<br>- Submitted to the Division of Criminal Justice and to the FBI for national background check|
|**PROHIBITED PRACTICES**|   |
|**Stranger-Originated Life Insurance (STOLI)**|- A person with no insurable interest originates a life insurance contract on another person;<br>- STOLIs are prohibited because they violate the concept of insurable interest;<br>- Life settlement contracts are not STOLIs|
|**Prohibited Practices**|- Intentional deception or fraud;<br>- Avoiding disclosure of material information;<br>- Receiving consideration other than normal commissions or fees;<br>- Acting as a life settlement broker or intermediary while having an interest that policy (conflict of interest);<br>- Transacting life settlements without a proper license;<br>- Not remitting proceeds required by contract to the owner;<br>- Paying finder's fees or any other compensation to any owner's physician, attorney, accountant, insurance producer or consultant, or other person acting in these capacities;<br>- Paying life settlement broker's fees before they have been fully disclosed; and<br>- Paying life settlement payments in installments.|
