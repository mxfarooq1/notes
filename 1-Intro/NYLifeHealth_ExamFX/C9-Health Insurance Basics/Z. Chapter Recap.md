This chapter explained the basics of health insurance and underwriting procedures for health insurance policies. Let's recap them:

|   |   |
|---|---|
|****TYPES OF LOSSES AND EXCLUSIONS****|   |
|****Principal Types of Losses and Benefits  <br>****|- _Disability loss of income_ - caused by insured's inability to work<br>- _Medical -_ medical or hospital expenses incurred from an accident or sickness<br>- _Dental -_ usually stand-alone plans; cover diagnostic and preventive care<br>- _Long-term care -_ covers expenses for care in a nursing home or in the insured's own home|
|**Classes of Health Insurance Policies**|- Individual vs. group<br>- Private vs. government<br>- Limited vs. comprehensive|
|**Types of Limited Policies**|- _Accident -_ coverage for disability, medical care, death or dismemberment resulting from an accident<br>- _Dread disease policy -_ variety of benefits for a specific disease such as cancer policy or heart disease policy<br>- _Critical illness -_ pays a lump sum to the insured upon diagnosis and survival of a critical illness<br>- _Hospital indemnity -_ provides a specific amount on a daily, weekly or monthly basis while the insured is confined to a hospital<br>- _Dental plans_ - covers the treatment, care and prevention of dental disease and injury to the insured's teeth<br>- _Vision/hearing plans_ - type of group health insurance that covers eye examinations and eyeglasses, or hearing aids on a limited basis|
|**Common Exclusions**|- Injuries resulting from war or active military service<br>- Intentionally self-inflicted injuries<br>- Elective cosmetic surgery<br>- Experimental procedures<br>- Conditions covered by workers compensation insurance<br>- Expenses paid for by government plans<br>- Injuries caused by participation in criminal activity|
|**REPLACEMENT**|   |
|**Agent’s Responsibility**|- Compare benefits, limitations and exclusions found in the current and the proposed replacement policy<br>- Provide Notice Regarding Replacement<br>- Ensure that the current policy is not cancelled before the new policy is issued|
