This chapter explained the key principles of group health insurance. Let's recap some of the important points:

|   |   |
|---|---|
|**GROUP INSURANCE**|   |
|**Characteristics of Group Insurance**|- Group formed for a purpose other than obtaining group health insurance<br>- _Master policy_ - issued to the group sponsor<br>- _Certificates of insurance_ - evidence of coverage for the insureds<br>- Experience rating: premium based on group as whole<br>- Community rating: premium based on insurer claims experience|
|**Types of Eligible Groups**|- _Employer sponsored_ - individual or Multiple Employer Trust (MET)<br>- _Association_ - alumni or professional|
|**Marketing Consideration**|- _Advertising_ - truthful and not misleading<br>- _Jurisdiction_ - coverage for more than one state; approved in issuing state|
|**Underwriting**|- Every eligible member of group must be covered regardless of physical condition, age, sex or occupation<br>- Evidence of insurability generally not required<br>- 30-day open enrollment period<br>- Probationary employees must work a total of 30 hours per week|
|**Provisions**|- Conversion to individual coverage - within 31 days without evidence of insurability<br>- Coordination of benefits<br>- Change of insurers - carryover of coinsurance and deductibles|
|**COBRA**|   |
|**Qualifying Events**|- Voluntary termination of employment<br>- Termination of employment for reasons other than gross misconduct (e.g., company downsizing)<br>- Employment status change from full time to part time|
|**Length of coverage**|- 18 months – after qualifying event<br>- 36 months – for dependents after events such as death of the employee, divorce or legal separation|
