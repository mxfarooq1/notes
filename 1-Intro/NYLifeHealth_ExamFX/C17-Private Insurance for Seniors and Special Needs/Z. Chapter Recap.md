This chapter explained Medicare Supplement policies and other Medicare options, including state regulations. Let's recap some of the key points:

|   |   |
|---|---|
|**MEDICARE SUPPLEMENT POLICIES**|   |
|**Basics**|- Referred to as Medigap<br>- Policies issued by private insurance companies to fill in gaps in Medicare<br>- Open enrollment period of 6-months|
|**Coverage**|- Plan A: core benefits, such as coinsurance/copayment; additional Part A hospital costs; hospice care coinsurance / copayment; Part B coinsurance/copayment; 3 pints of blood under Parts A and B<br>- Plans B – N: core benefits + various additional benefits|
|**State Regulations**|- Standards for marketing for insurers<br>- Prohibition of high-pressure tactics and misleading advertising<br>- Suitability of recommendations<br>- No more than one Medicare supplement policy per insured<br>- Agent's compensation: first year commission cannot be more than 200% of the renewal commission for servicing the policy in the second year<br>- Replacement and disclosure requirements|
|**OTHER OPTIONS FOR INDIVIDUALS WITH MEDICARE**|   |
|**Employer Group Health Plans**|- Disabled employees: coverage for disabled individuals under age 65<br>- Employees with kidney failure: primary coverage for 30 months for end-stage renal disease, prior to Medicare<br>- Individual age 65 or older:<br>    - Requires employer to continue offering coverage if Medicare is deferred<br>    - Employer cannot offer incentives for switching plan<br>    - Medicare is primary if employer has no more than 20 employees|
